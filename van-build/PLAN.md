# 2009 Honda Odyssey → Mobile Detailing Van

Build plan for converting the seats-out Odyssey into a working mobile detailing rig:
water tank, pressure washer, the Giraffe retractable reel (finally mounted properly),
and wood-framed bin racks.

**Interactive 3D model:** open [`odyssey-3d.html`](odyssey-3d.html) in a browser —
drag to orbit, scroll to zoom, toggle layers on/off.

> All dimensions are nominal for a 3rd-gen (2005–2010) Odyssey with the 2nd and 3rd
> rows removed. Measure your actual van before cutting anything.

---

## 1. Weight budget (do this first)

A 2009 Odyssey carries roughly **1,300 lb of payload** — the exact number is on your
driver-door-jamb sticker; use that one. Removing the 2nd and 3rd row seats gives back
about 180–200 lb.

| Item | Notes | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Water, 35 gal, full | 8.34 lb/gal — the big one | 292 lb |
| Tank + fittings | poly leg tank | ~35 lb |
| Pressure washer | typical 2.5–3 GPM gas unit | ~65 lb |
| Plywood deck + framing | ¾″ ply, 2×4 sleepers, 2×2 racks | ~120 lb |
| Giraffe reel + hoses | reel, supply hose, pressure hose | ~45 lb |
| Bins, chemicals, buckets, vacuum, tools | varies | ~150 lb |
| Driver | | ~180 lb |
| **Total** | | **~890 lb** |

This is why the plan says **35 gallons, not 65**: a full 65-gal tank is 542 lb of
water and eats the entire budget. Refill between jobs, or step up to 50 gal (417 lb)
only if you strip weight elsewhere. Keep the tank just behind the front seats,
centered, so the mass rides **between the axles**, not behind the rear one.

## 2. The floor: one plywood deck

The seats-out floor isn't flat (the 3rd-row "magic seat" tub is a deep well) and the
floor pan is thin ribbed sheet metal you should never screw into. Build one deck that
everything else bolts to:

1. Frame the 3rd-row tub with **2×4 sleepers on edge**, level with the main floor.
2. Cap the cargo area with **¾″ exterior-grade plywood** (~78″ × 48″ usable between
   the wheel wells, notched around them). Seal with porch paint or spar urethane —
   this floor lives wet.
3. **Anchor the deck to the factory seat-latch points.** The Odyssey's 2nd/3rd-row
   seat strikers are welded to the frame and rated for crash loads. Bolt through them
   with 5⁄16″ (M8) hardware and fender washers.
4. Install **forged eye bolts** at four of those anchor points, sticking up through
   the deck — these are the tie-down points for the tank straps.
5. Never rely on self-tapping screws into the floor pan: they strip, leak, and let go
   in a hard stop.

## 3. Water tank: 35-gal poly leg tank

- Low-profile **leg tank** (~38″ × 24″ × 17″ for 35 gal); the molded feet have strap
  grooves.
- Position: centered, immediately behind the front seats.
- Secure with **two 2″ ratchet straps** (rated ≥ 3,300 lb each) over the strap
  grooves down to the eye bolts. Rated straps, not bungees — a full tank in a 30 mph
  stop carries tons of force.
- Plumbing: ¾″ bulkhead fitting low on the rear face → ball valve → 100-mesh inline
  filter → garden-hose thread to the pressure washer inlet.
- Add a fill port reachable through the sliding door and a vent (loosely-capped lid
  works).
- Half-full tanks slosh hard; run full or near-empty when you can.

## 4. The Giraffe reel — the fix

**Stop trying to mount it to the van. Build it a wall.**

The Giraffe bracket is designed to lag into wall studs — four screws deep into solid
lumber. The van gives you nothing like that: plastic trim over air, and body skin that
flexes and tears out. Bring the wall with you:

1. **Build a mounting wall:** two vertical **2×4 studs, 16″ apart**, bolted at the
   bottom through the plywood deck into the rear seat-anchor hardware, faced with
   **¾″ plywood**. Tie the top to a side-wall rib with one bracket (or run a 2×4
   header to a matching post — zero holes in the van body).
2. **Location:** driver side, just ahead of the tailgate, **facing rearward** — the
   Giraffe pivots 180°, so the hose pays straight out the open hatch and the
   auto-rewind pulls straight back. No dragging around door edges.
3. **Through-bolt, don't screw:** mount the bracket with **5⁄16″ bolts + fender
   washers + nylock nuts** through the plywood face. The rewind spring yanks that
   bracket on every retract; through-bolts never work loose the way wood screws do.
4. **Height:** center the bracket ~34–36″ above the deck so the reel clears the shelf
   below and the hose exits level with the tailgate opening.

Parts: two 2×4s, ~2 ft² of ¾″ ply, four carriage bolts (base), four through-bolts
(bracket). One afternoon.

## 5. Pressure washer: slide-out tray at the tailgate

⚠️ **If it's gas-powered, never run it inside the van or at the open hatch.** Carbon
monoxide plus a hot muffler in a metal box is how conversions burn down.

- Mount the washer on a **250-lb locking drawer-slide tray** (or a simple lift-out
  plywood tray with handles) at the rear passenger corner. Slide it past the bumper
  or set it on the ground to run.
- Locking pins or a strap hold it during transport; **quick-connects** on the water
  feed and pressure hose make deploy/stow a 60-second job.
- Electric alternative: a ~2,000 PSI electric unit can run from the tray with the
  hatch open. Many mobile detailers carry gas for driveways, electric for garages.
- Gas cans ride in a vented plastic tray, never loose.

## 6. Storage: wood-framed bin racks

The same trick as the reel wall — the frame bolts to the deck, not the van:

- **2×2 uprights** lag-bolted to the deck, ~24″ bays, **½″ ply shelves** at 13″ and
  24″ above the deck. Keep racks ≤ 12″ deep so the aisle stays open.
- **1×2 lip** along every shelf edge + a bungee across each bay so bins survive the
  ferry ramp.
- Driver side: chemicals and pads (forward of the reel wall). Passenger side: towels,
  buckets, vacuum (forward of the washer tray).
- Cap each wheel-well top with a ply shelf — free real estate for grab-most items.

## 7. Plumbing & power

Water path: **tank → ball valve → filter → washer inlet** (most 2.5 GPM washers
gravity-feed fine from a tank sitting above the inlet). The Giraffe reel carries the
rinse/supply hose.

Optional but worth it: a **12V diaphragm pump + accumulator** teed off the tank for
rinseless washes and bucket filling without starting the washer. Feed it from a
**50 Ah deep-cycle battery** behind the driver's seat (with an isolator to the van
battery); it also runs work lights and charges the vacuum.

## 9. Build order

- [ ] **Prep** — seats out, carpet out (it never dries), find all seat-anchor points,
      measure the actual van.
- [ ] **Deck** — frame the tub, cut/seal/bolt the plywood, install the four eye bolts.
- [ ] **Reel wall** — build, bolt to deck + rear anchors, through-bolt the Giraffe
      bracket, test pull-out and rewind with the hatch open.
- [ ] **Tank** — set, strap, plumb bulkhead → valve → filter.
- [ ] **Washer tray** — build slide-out/lift-out, add quick-connects and transport
      strap.
- [ ] **Racks** — frames, shelves, lips, bungees; load bins and shake-test.
- [ ] **Shake-down** — full tank, bumpiest regular route, re-torque every bolt after
      the first week.
